Nintendo Plastic Playing were developed in nineteen fifty-three. The development of the Plastic Playing cards were sixty years after the Nintendo company started. Nintendo was the first company to mass produce the Plastic Playing cards in Japan. Hiroshi Yamauchi was the president of Nintendo for four years when the Plastic Playing milestone occurred. The production of the Plastic Playing cards took time and effort. The printing of the Plastic Playing cards was difficult to accomplish because it was not as easy to print on plastic. The Plastic Playing cards were also called NAP which stands for Nintendo All Plastic. The Plastic Playing cards were a new idea playing card industry they were different they were not traditional. The Plastic Playing cards were said to be longer lasting and sanitary compared to other cards. In the beginning of the Plastic Playing card development there were four designs of the cards. Now there are multiple designs of the cards. The selling factor of the Plastic Playing cards was that they were washable. Plastic Playing cards were the first ever cards that were able to be washed. The Plastic Playing cards were more durable which meant they were expensive to make. Although the price of Plastic Playing cards were higher in price than regular playing cards, people were still willing to play for the Plastic Playing cards because of their high quality. Nintendo also produce paper playing cards for a cheaper option that was aimed for children. The Plastic Playing cards were still preferred over the cheaper paper playing cards.
