Famicom

Nintendo released the Famicom in nineteen eighty-three in Japan. The Famicom was also known as the FamilyComputer. Famicom was going to be named the GameCom but the main designers wife suggested the name Famicom and they decided to name the game console Famicam. Two years after the release of the Famicom in Japan it was then released in October in the year eighty-five. Nintendo developed the Famicom to bring gaming to the household. Most people were playing video games in the arcade, so Nintendo wanted to develop something new and diverse. The design of the Famicom came from an invention that was also developed by Nintendo. Nintendo reused the design but gave it many new add-ons to better the development of the Famicom. Although the designers reused the design they still wanted The Famicom to be unique in its design and different from other gaming consoles. When the Famicom was invented, it was a popular selling gaming console. Famicom changed the ways of gameplay and made a gateway for future inventions. The designers of Nintendo when designing the Famicom wanted to produce it and sell it without using to much money. The Famicom was sold in bundles. There were various bundles that the Famicom were sold in. There were four bundles for the Famicom there was the deluxe, the basic, the action and the power sets. The sets came several types of game controllers and video games depending on which set you bought. In two thousand and three Nintendo decided to discontinue the Famicom in North America and Europe.
